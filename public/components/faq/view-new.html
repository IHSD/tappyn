Launching A Campaign

Writing an on-point brief is the first key to success when runnign a messaging campaign. An effective breif gives creators everything they need to know to exceed your expectations. Think about how much experience you have briefing and working with creators and how targeted your messaging must be. The more you put into your campaign, the more you'll get out.

Entering A Campaign

After logging in, simply go to the create tab and click the button, 'Submit.' Once you've entered into the campaign, you're all set. Do NOT submit stock images or stock art. Clients wouldn't be happy if they are legally sued over copyright issues arising from your work - and you'll be liable for any costs and damages they incur. Create messages for as many campaigns that interest you! We've had users get paid for multiple campaigns in a week, so we recommend that you create messages for all of them.

How Campaigns Work

Campaigns will usually run for 7 days. A customer can end a campaign at any time by selecting their favorite, but if they don't, the contest will run in 3 stages:

Creative: The first 4 days of the contest are the Creative. During this stage, the campaign is open to all eligible creators, and listed on our campaign listings page. This is the creators chance to show off their skills, and the campaign holder’s chance to see how creator are interpreting their brief.

Testing: At the end of the Creative, the campaign holder will have up to 2 days to edit and select the messages they'd like to test.

Selected: After testing ends, the campaign holder has up to 1 week to review and select their favorite messages to own. Testing is the second stage of your campaign and it begins as soon as you click 'Start' from your dashboard. We take the messages you've selected and run them as advertisements on other channels using the same targeting you gave us for your campaign.

We'll see how the messages compare to one another and then show you the rankings of each numbered 1 - 30. You can see the campaign status that your message was submitted to right in your dashboard. Here's a breakdown of what they mean...

-Live: This means that a campaign hasn't ended yet, and more messages are in the process of being created. Once a campaign reaches its cap of 30 messages or time runs out, it will be ready to start testing.

-Pending Testing: The campaign has ended and we're about to start testing your messages in real time on a live platform.

-Testing: Selected messages are tested for approximately 24 hours so both you and the company hosting the campaign can see how they perform.

-Pending Selection: Testing has been completed, and you should be able to see the CTR (Click Through Rate) that your message generated in your dashboard.

-Complete: This final status means that the campaign has ended, testing is done, and the company that hosted the campaign has purchased their favorite messages.
