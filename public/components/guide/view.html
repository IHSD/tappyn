Writing a Copy

You don't have to be a novelist to write effective text for a submission. Following a few simple tips can help make your text more effective and consistent in your submissions. More importantly, if you do nothing else, spellcheck.

1. Tone of Voice

High-end Restaurant

Sports-themed Restaurant

Family-friendly Restaurant

Start by considering your tone of voice, which can simple be a reflection of your individual personality. Is it funny? All business? Adventurous? Every person has their own identity and the more authentic you are, the more effective your submissions are likely to be. Be yourself and consistent.

2. Stick to What's Important

Probably Too Long

Probably Just Right

Remember, people scroll through feeds quickly, so the chances they're going to stop and read a novel are slim. Stick to the important information and pay attention to the character count limit. Try to get the good stuff in before the text is truncated. And if you have a specific action you want, ask for it. Visit our website. Call for a free quote. Install our app. Etc.

3. Write with yourself in mind

What motivates you?

Address their concerns (like prices)

Entertain them

Consider talking about things in terms of your own mindset and what might appeal to people like you emotionally. People spend most of their time thinking about their own lives, so put yourself in their shoes and think "what would make me like this business?"
